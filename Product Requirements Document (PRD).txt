**1\. Executive Summary**

**1.1 Product Vision**

Build a comprehensive forensic analysis system that accurately detects AI-generated human images through multi-modal technical analysis, providing explainable results to combat misinformation, identity fraud, and deepfake content.

**1.2 Product Overview**

The AI Image Authenticity Detector is a hybrid forensic system combining:

- **Physics-based analysis** (lighting, shadows, reflections)
- **Statistical image analysis** (noise patterns, frequency domain)
- **Camera sensor analysis** (PRNU fingerprinting)
- **Facial consistency checks** (anatomical correctness)
- **Metadata inspection** (EXIF data validation)
- **AI-based classifier** (trained CNN model)

**1.3 Business Objectives**

- Achieve **85%+ accuracy** in detecting AI-generated human images
- Provide **explainable detection results** with confidence scores
- Process images in **under 5 seconds** per analysis
- Support **API integration** for third-party platforms
- Build a **scalable solution** that adapts to evolving AI generation techniques

**1.4 Target Market**

- Social media platforms (content moderation)
- Law enforcement agencies (digital forensics)
- News organizations (fact-checking)
- Financial institutions (KYC verification)
- Dating platforms (profile verification)
- Legal professionals (evidence authentication)

**2\. Problem Statement**

**2.1 Current Challenges**

Modern AI image generation tools (Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, Midjourney, etc.) create photorealistic human images that are visually indistinguishable from real photographs. This technological advancement enables:

- **Fake social media profiles** using synthetic identities
- **Identity theft and fraud** with convincing fake documentation
- **Misinformation campaigns** using fabricated imagery
- **Deepfake content distribution** undermining trust
- **Unauthorized use of synthetic identities** in financial crimes

**2.2 Why Current Solutions Fall Short**

- **Single-method detectors** are easily bypassed by new AI models
- **Black-box AI classifiers** lack explainability for legal/forensic use
- **Visual inspection** is unreliable even for experts
- **Metadata-only checks** can be easily forged or stripped
- **Rapid AI evolution** makes detection a moving target

**2.3 Our Solution**

A **multi-forensic hybrid system** that:

- Combines 7 independent forensic modules + 1 AI classifier
- Provides explainable, weighted fusion of evidence
- Adapts through continuous model retraining
- Operates at pixel and frequency levels beyond human perception
- Delivers court-admissible forensic reports

**F2: PRNU (Photo Response Non-Uniformity) Detection**

**Priority:** P0 (Must Have)  
**Module:** detectors/prnu_detector.py

**Background:** Every camera sensor has a unique noise pattern (fingerprint) caused by manufacturing imperfections. Real photos contain this fingerprint; AI-generated images do not.

**Requirements:**

- Extract sensor noise fingerprint from image
- Apply high-pass filtering to isolate noise from content
- Use wavelet-based denoising (DWT)
- Calculate noise residual patterns
- Compare against expected sensor noise characteristics
- Generate PRNU confidence score (0.0 = no sensor noise, 1.0 = strong sensor noise)

**Algorithm:**

1\. Convert image to grayscale

2\. Apply high-pass filter (removes content, keeps noise)

3\. Apply wavelet denoising

4\. Extract noise residual

5\. Calculate entropy and randomness of residual

6\. Compare to reference sensor noise patterns

7\. Output PRNU score

**Implementation Details:**

- **File:** detectors/prnu_detector.py
  - extract_noise_residual(image): High-pass filtering + wavelet denoising
  - calculate_prnu_score(residual): Entropy and pattern analysis
  - detect_sensor_fingerprint(image): Main detection function

**Expected Outputs:**

- DSLR images: PRNU score 0.7-0.95
- Smartphone images: PRNU score 0.5-0.8
- AI-generated images: PRNU score 0.0-0.3

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- DSLR images score >0.7 on average
- AI-generated images score <0.3 on average
- Processing time <2 seconds per image
- Clear separation between real and AI distributions

**Dependencies:**

- NumPy, SciPy (signal processing)
- PyWavelets (wavelet transforms)

**F3: Pixel Noise Statistical Analysis**

**Priority:** P0 (Must Have)  
**Module:** detectors/pixel_noise.py

**Background:** Natural camera noise is random and chaotic. AI-generated images often have artificially smooth or patterned noise.

**Requirements:**

- Analyze pixel-level noise distribution
- Calculate statistical measures:
  - Entropy (randomness)
  - Standard deviation
  - Uniformity
  - Autocorrelation
- Detect artificial smoothness or repetitive patterns
- Generate noise naturalness score (0.0 = artificial, 1.0 = natural)

**Algorithm:**

1\. Extract high-frequency noise component

2\. Calculate entropy across color channels

3\. Compute spatial autocorrelation

4\. Measure uniformity of noise distribution

5\. Compare to natural noise characteristics

6\. Output naturalness score

**Implementation Details:**

- **File:** detectors/pixel_noise.py
  - extract_noise(image): High-frequency component extraction
  - calculate_entropy(noise): Shannon entropy
  - analyze_uniformity(noise): Distribution analysis
  - compute_naturalness_score(image): Main scoring function

**Expected Outputs:**

- Real images: Naturalness score 0.6-0.95
- AI images (smooth): Naturalness score 0.1-0.4
- Heavily compressed real images: 0.4-0.7

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- Natural noise patterns score >0.6
- Artificial patterns score <0.4
- Analysis completes in <1 second
- Robust to JPEG compression artifacts

**F4: Frequency Domain Analysis (FFT)**

**Priority:** P0 (Must Have)  
**Module:** detectors/frequency_analysis.py

**Background:** AI generation models (especially diffusion models and GANs) leave characteristic frequency-domain signatures. Real camera images have smooth frequency decay.

**Requirements:**

- Perform 2D Fast Fourier Transform (FFT)
- Analyze frequency spectrum patterns
- Detect periodic artifacts (grid patterns, repetitive frequencies)
- Identify GAN/diffusion model signatures:
  - Checkerboard artifacts
  - Spectral peaks at specific frequencies
  - Unnatural frequency concentrations
- Generate frequency artifact score (0.0 = clean, 1.0 = artifacts detected)

**Algorithm:**

1\. Convert image to grayscale

2\. Apply 2D FFT

3\. Compute magnitude spectrum

4\. Normalize and log-scale

5\. Detect periodic peaks

6\. Compare to expected natural frequency decay

7\. Output artifact score

**Implementation Details:**

- **File:** detectors/frequency_analysis.py
  - compute_fft(image): 2D FFT computation
  - analyze_spectrum(fft_result): Peak detection
  - detect_periodic_artifacts(spectrum): Grid/pattern detection
  - calculate_artifact_score(image): Main scoring function

**Expected Outputs:**

- Real images: Artifact score 0.0-0.3
- Diffusion model images: Artifact score 0.6-0.9
- GAN images: Artifact score 0.5-0.85

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- Diffusion model artifacts detected with >75% accuracy
- Real images show smooth frequency decay
- Analysis completes in <1.5 seconds
- Visualization of frequency spectrum available for debugging

**Dependencies:**

- NumPy (FFT functions)
- SciPy (signal processing)
- Matplotlib (optional, for visualization)

**F5: Shadow Geometry Analysis**

**Priority:** P1 (Should Have)  
**Module:** detectors/shadow_geometry.py

**Background:** In physically consistent lighting, all shadows should point away from a single light source. AI models sometimes generate inconsistent shadow directions.

**Requirements:**

- Detect facial shadows (nose, cheek, chin, neck)
- Calculate shadow direction vectors
- Verify consistency with single light source hypothesis
- Check for physically impossible shadows:
  - Multiple conflicting directions
  - Missing expected shadows
  - Shadows inconsistent with highlights
- Generate shadow consistency score (0.0 = inconsistent, 1.0 = consistent)

**Algorithm:**

1\. Detect face and facial landmarks

2\. Identify shadow regions using edge detection and intensity analysis

3\. Calculate gradient directions in shadow areas

4\. Estimate light source position from shadow vectors

5\. Check consistency across multiple shadow locations

6\. Output consistency score

**Implementation Details:**

- **File:** detectors/shadow_geometry.py
  - detect_shadows(image, face_landmarks): Shadow region detection
  - calculate_shadow_vectors(shadows): Direction computation
  - estimate_light_source(vectors): Light position estimation
  - verify_consistency(light_source, shadows): Consistency check
  - compute_shadow_score(image): Main scoring function

**Expected Outputs:**

- Physically consistent shadows: Score 0.7-1.0
- Minor inconsistencies: Score 0.4-0.7
- Major inconsistencies (AI artifact): Score 0.0-0.4

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- Consistent shadows score >0.7
- Inconsistent shadows detected and scored <0.4
- Works under various lighting conditions (front, side, top)
- Gracefully handles images without clear shadows

**Limitations:**

- May produce false positives in complex multi-light scenarios
- Less reliable in evenly lit (studio) conditions
- Requires clear facial shadows to be effective

**F7: Facial Inconsistency Detection**

**Priority:** P0 (Must Have)

  - Ear structure and position

  - Unnaturally perfect teeth alignment

  - Mismatched earrings or accessories

**Algorithm:**

1\. Detect facial landmarks

2\. Calculate symmetry metrics:

\- Distance ratios (eye-to-eye, nose-to-mouth, etc.)

\- Shape comparisons (left vs right eye)

\- Alignment checks

3\. Compare to biological norms and variance

4\. Flag extreme symmetry (AI artifact)

5\. Output consistency score

**Implementation Details:**

- **File:** detectors/facial_consistency.py
  - extract_landmarks(image): 68-point landmark detection
  - calculate_symmetry_metrics(landmarks): Bilateral comparison
  - check_anatomical_correctness(landmarks): Norm validation
  - detect_perfect_symmetry(metrics): Suspiciously perfect features
  - compute_facial_score(image): Main scoring function

**Expected Outputs:**

- Real faces (natural asymmetry): Score 0.6-0.9
- AI faces (over-symmetry or inconsistencies): Score 0.2-0.5
- Extreme AI artifacts: Score 0.0-0.3

**Acceptance Criteria:**

- Asymmetry >20% in key features triggers low score
- Perfect symmetry (AI indicator) triggers suspicion
- Analysis completes in <1 second
- Robust to head rotation (±15 degrees)

**Dependencies:**

- MediaPipe or dlib (landmark detection)
- NumPy (geometric calculations)

**F10: Decision Fusion Engine**

**Priority:** P0 (Must Have)  
**Module:** fusion/decision_engine.py

**Background:** Combines scores from all forensic modules using weighted fusion to produce final verdict and confidence score.

**Requirements:**

- Accept scores from all 7 forensic modules + AI model
- Apply weighted combination formula
- Generate final classification: **Real**, **AI-Generated**, or **Uncertain**
- Calculate overall confidence percentage
- Provide detailed reasoning breakdown
- Support configurable weights for different use cases

**Fusion Formula:**

Final Score =

w1 × PRNU +

w2 × Frequency +

w3 × Pixel Noise +

w4 × Facial Consistency +

w5 × Shadow +

w6 × Reflection +

w7 × Metadata +

w8 × AI Model

Default Weights:

w1 = 0.20 (PRNU - strong indicator)

w2 = 0.15 (Frequency)

w3 = 0.10 (Pixel Noise)

w4 = 0.10 (Facial)

w5 = 0.10 (Shadow)

w6 = 0.10 (Reflection)

w7 = 0.05 (Metadata - easily forged)

w8 = 0.20 (AI Model - learned patterns)

**Classification Thresholds:**

- **AI-Generated:** Final Score < 0.40
- **Uncertain:** 0.40 ≤ Final Score ≤ 0.60
- **Real:** Final Score > 0.60

**Reasoning Logic:** Generate natural language explanation based on strongest indicators:

- If PRNU < 0.3: "No camera sensor noise detected"
- If Frequency > 0.7: "Frequency artifacts characteristic of AI generation"
- If Facial < 0.4: "Facial inconsistencies or unnatural symmetry"
- If AI Model > 0.8: "Strong AI fingerprint detected by neural network"

**Implementation Details:**

- **File:** fusion/decision_engine.py
  - DecisionEngine(weights=default_weights): Initialize with weights
  - fuse_scores(scores_dict): Weighted combination
  - classify(final_score): Apply thresholds
  - generate_reasoning(scores_dict, verdict): Explanation generation
  - compute_confidence(final_score): Confidence percentage
  - analyze(scores_dict): Main analysis function

**Output Format:**

\]

}

**Acceptance Criteria:**

**17\. Appendices**

**Appendix A: Glossary of Terms**

**AI-Generated Image:** An image created by an artificial intelligence model (e.g., Stable Diffusion, DALL-E) rather than captured by a camera.

**PRNU (Photo Response Non-Uniformity):** A unique noise pattern inherent to every camera sensor, acting as a digital fingerprint.

**FFT (Fast Fourier Transform):** Mathematical transformation that converts spatial domain image data into frequency domain, revealing periodic patterns.

**Diffusion Model:** A type of AI image generation model that starts with noise and iteratively refines it into a coherent image (e.g., Stable Diffusion).

**GAN (Generative Adversarial Network):** A type of AI model consisting of a generator and discriminator that compete to create realistic images.

**EXIF Metadata:** Exchangeable Image File Format data embedded in images, containing camera settings and other information.

**Deepfake:** Synthetic media created using AI, often depicting events or people in fabricated scenarios.

**Catchlight:** The reflection of light sources visible in a subject's eyes, useful for analyzing lighting consistency.

**Specular Reflection:** Mirror-like reflection of light from shiny surfaces (e.g., skin, eyes).

**Facial Landmarks:** Specific points on a face (e.g., eye corners, nose tip) used for analysis and recognition.

**Appendix B: Algorithm Descriptions**

**PRNU Extraction:**

- Apply high-pass filter to remove image content
- Use wavelet-based denoising (DWT)
- Extract noise residual
- Calculate statistical properties (entropy, variance)
- Compare to expected sensor noise characteristics

**Frequency Analysis (FFT):**

- Convert image to grayscale
- Apply 2D Fast Fourier Transform
- Compute magnitude spectrum
- Detect periodic peaks (indicative of AI artifacts)
- Analyze frequency decay pattern

**Shadow Geometry:**

- Detect facial landmarks
- Identify shadow regions using edge detection
- Calculate gradient directions in shadows
- Estimate light source position
- Verify consistency across multiple shadows
